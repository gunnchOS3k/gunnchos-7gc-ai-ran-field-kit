# Abstract (≤250 words)

**Title:** Resilience-Aware, Human-Centric AI-RAN Orchestration for Service Continuity Across Terrestrial and Non-Terrestrial Networks  
**Author:** Edmund Gunn Jr.  
**Date:** 2026-07-24

---

Affordable edge users in heterogeneous environments need wireless systems that preserve service continuity when terrestrial coverage degrades and when non-terrestrial or local-edge fallbacks introduce latency, energy, and privacy costs. This doctoral research in Communications Engineering investigates whether a privacy-preserving orchestration architecture—combining controlled device measurements, workload intent, digital-twin context, and multi-access network state—can improve continuity and worst-user quality of experience versus static, network-only, and service-priority baselines under explicit fairness, energy, privacy, and reliability constraints.

Three research questions structure the work: (RQ1) trustworthy construction of reproducible twin state without direct identifiers; (RQ2) twin-informed AI-RAN orchestration across terrestrial and local-edge conditions with preregistered baselines and holdouts; and (RQ3) conditions under which terrestrial, offline, local-edge, and NTN fallback policies succeed or fail under measurement uncertainty.

The methodology integrates schema-validated pipelines from Edge-IO measurement through digital-twin state to AI-RAN and NTN resilience modules, a preregistered 54-session local pilot matrix, and source-validated simulation for failure-boundary analysis. System integration is complete; authentic eligible pilot sessions remain 0/54 pending approved zones and dates. Evaluation design is preregistered with primary outcome recovery time; scientific evaluation on complete physical data is not yet executed.

Expected contributions are reproducible measurement and twin contracts, human-centric orchestration policies with honest uncertainty reporting, and documented multi-access failure boundaries. The work does not claim finished commercial products, deployment-scale 6G, or global generalization from a single pilot site.
